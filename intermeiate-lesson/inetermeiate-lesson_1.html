"What is your name?"

What does "What is your name?" Mean?

"What is your name?" is a simple and essential question commonly used during introductions. By answering this question, you can introduce yourself.

Question Structure

The structure of "What is your name?" is as follows:

  • What: A question word meaning "What?"
  • is: The present tense of the verb "to be" (used with singular subjects).
  • your: Possessive pronoun meaning "Your."
  • name: Noun meaning "Name."

When put together: "What is your name?" → "What is your name?"

Answering the Question

To answer this question, use the following formula:

  • My name is [name]. (For example: "My name is John.")

Examples:

  • What is your name? → My name is John.
  • What is your name? → My name is Sarah.

Shortened Form

The shortened form of "What is your name?" is: "What's your name?". The meaning remains the same and is more commonly used in informal conversations.

Example:

  • What's your name? → My name is Tom.

Tips

To master using this question and answering it:

  • Practice introducing yourself.
  • Memorize the sentences with proper pronunciation.
  • Plan how you will answer in conversations.
